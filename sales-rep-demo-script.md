# Numat AI Sales Assistant Demo Script

## Goal Of The Demo

Show the sales team what the app already does, then use their feedback to shape the next phase.

The aim is not to present a finished AI product. The aim is to show a useful working tool and invite the team to help decide what should come next.

## Suggested Demo Flow

### 1. Start with the home page

Suggested intro:

“What we have built so far is a way to highlight customers who may need attention, show recent customer context, and help prioritize follow-up.”

What to show:

- summary tiles
- Today’s Action Plan
- Hold / Recently Contacted

What to say:

- the app is already grouping follow-up work into something practical
- it is trying to reduce duplicate outreach
- it is meant to support rep judgement, not replace it

### 2. Show Customers Needing Attention

What to show:

- priority scores
- average cycle
- days since last order
- explanation column
- recent activity context

Suggested talking point:

“Here the app is looking at the customer’s normal order rhythm and highlighting accounts that look overdue compared with their usual pattern.”

Question to ask:

- “Does this reflect the kind of accounts you would actually want to review first?”

### 3. Show the Customers summary view

What to show:

- order counts
- CRM counts
- last order
- last activity
- average cycle
- total value

Suggested talking point:

“This is the account-level view, so before anyone reaches out they can quickly see what kind of customer this is and what has been happening.”

Question to ask:

- “Is this the right summary information, or is there something missing that you would want at this level?”

### 4. Drill into one customer

Pick a customer with:

- recent CRM activity
- several orders
- enough history to tell a story

What to show:

- customer summary tiles
- Last Contact
- CRM Timeline
- Contact Ready panel

Suggested talking point:

“This is where a rep can check the customer history before contacting them, instead of jumping between multiple systems.”

Question to ask:

- “If you were about to contact this customer, what else would you want to see here?”

### 5. Show CRM Activities briefly

What to show:

- CRM filter/search view
- recent communication previews

Suggested talking point:

“Behind the scenes we now have live CRM data feeding this, so the app is no longer just using sample order history.”

Question to ask:

- “Would you want to use this page directly, or would you rather CRM details stay mainly inside the customer view?”

## Transition Into Future Features

Suggested wording:

“Now that the app can already identify who may need attention and show the recent context, the next question is what would help you act on that information.”

Then offer likely next areas:

- outreach support
- suggested next action
- visit planning
- route planning
- customer briefing summaries

## Suggested Questions To Ask The Team

1. What would help you most after seeing a customer in the action plan?
2. What should happen next once a customer is flagged?
3. Would it be more useful to draft outreach or help plan visits?
4. What information would you want before going to see a customer?
5. What would make this useful every day rather than just interesting?

## Likely Feature Ideas To Listen For

You may hear requests around:

- follow-up email drafting
- call notes or talking points
- route planning for visits
- grouping nearby customers
- visit prep summaries
- reminders or rep task lists

## Suggested Closing Line

“What we want from this session is not just reaction to what is here now. We want to understand what would genuinely help you move from insight to action.”
